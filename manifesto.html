Manifesto for Integrated Co-Existing Mental Health and Substance Use Care

From Fragmentation to Integration: A Call for Systemic Reform

Why This Manifesto Matters

The failure to address co-existing mental health and substance use difficulties (CEMS) is one of the greatest systemic injustices of our time. Current care models remain fragmented, denying individuals the dignity, support, and holistic care they need.

This manifesto calls for a radical shift in policy, service provision, and societal understanding to end the cycle of exclusion and systemic neglect.

Core Principles for Systemic Change

  • Structural Reform: Integrating mental health and substance use services into a unified, person-centred system.
  • Social Determinants of Health: Addressing poverty, housing instability, and trauma as root causes.
  • Trauma-Informed Care: Rejecting punitive models in favour of compassionate, evidence-based support.
  • Dismantling Stigma: Ending professional silos and public misconceptions about addiction and mental health.
  • Empowerment & Harm Reduction: Prioritising autonomy, peer-led initiatives, and harm reduction interventions.
